Advocacy & Pioneering

Human Rights Defenders Advocacy

A lifelong commitment to protecting those who defend human rights. From founding the West African Human Rights Defenders' Network to coordinating Amnesty International's regional programs, Dr. Gassama has been at the forefront of the global movement to safeguard human rights defenders.

Pioneer & Founder

West African Human Rights Defenders' Network (ROADDH)

Pioneer and co-founder of ROADDH, created in 2005. The network covers 16 countries across West Africa—Mali, Mauritania, Guinea, Senegal, Gambia, Niger, Burkina Faso, Ivory Coast, Cape Verde, Sierra Leone, Liberia, Togo, Benin, Ghana, Nigeria, and Guinea Bissau—providing protection, advocacy, and solidarity for defenders across the region.

Education & Training

Applied Human Rights Academy

Translating over three decades of field experience into structured learning. The Academy offers online courses for scholars, practitioners, and anyone committed to understanding human rights in peacekeeping and conflict resolution.

Scholars and Practitioners Approach to Human Rights Online Learning

The course shares a wealth of knowledge and lived experiences to empower learners to contribute to social change. It demonstrates how the multilateral system is tested on human rights at field level, far beyond the conference halls of New York and Geneva.

Focus

Context-based learning with case studies from UN Peacekeeping and Political Field Missions across Africa. Scholars and practitioners translate theories and norms into field-level action.

Format

  • Self-paced online modules
  • Weekend lectures by Professors & Practitioners
  • Maximum 12 weeks
  • Graded Certification of Attendance

Assessment

  • Online participation & discussion (25%)
  • Recorded audio/video feedback (25%)
  • Final panel discussion (50%)

Course Modules

  1. Introduction to conflict drivers and dynamics in selected African conflict zones
  2. Introduction to the Protection of Civilians in armed conflict
  3. The UN-led Multilateral Agenda on Human Rights
  4. Human rights mandates for UN Peacekeeping and Political Field Missions
  5. Visions, strategies, and operational designs of human rights work in field missions
  6. Day-to-day management of human rights actions within UN Field Missions
  7. Transitional justice in the context of ongoing armed conflict
  8. Civic and political space in the context of ongoing armed conflict
  9. Accountability in the context of ongoing armed conflict
